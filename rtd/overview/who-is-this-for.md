# Who MIMB is for

Mastodon is My Blog (MIMB) is for people who already like Mastodon, but do **not** like how much effort it can take to read well inside a fast-moving stream.

## A good fit

MIMB is a good fit if you:

- treat Mastodon as a place for thoughtful writing, not just quick reactions
- want to read people one at a time instead of in a giant blended timeline
- follow niche topics and wish Mastodon handled them better
- want local control instead of handing everything to a third-party hosted dashboard
- manage more than one Mastodon identity

## Especially useful for

### Personal bloggers

If your posts are closer to mini-essays, note collections, or self-threaded writing, MIMB's **Storms** view will probably feel natural.

### Curators and link collectors

If you like finding software, articles, jobs, or images through people you trust, the **Content** area is one of MIMB's best ideas.

### Multi-account Mastodon users

If you split your life across a main account, art account, work account, or interest-specific accounts, MIMB can keep them under one local setup.

## Probably not a good fit

MIMB may not be the right tool if you want:

- a fully hosted, no-install service
- the exact same experience as your Mastodon server
- every Mastodon feature in one place today
- a polished social media team workflow

## Current maturity

MIMB is usable, but still clearly growing. Expect a mix of:

- solid core ideas
- practical local workflows
- some rough edges
- some pages and filters that are still improving

That is exactly why this handbook focuses on **what to expect**, not just what buttons exist.
